# LEELA Studio — Agent Guidelines (v2.0)

## Core Philosophy
1. **Goal**: Create original cinematic storytelling with feature-film production quality.
2. **Artistic Integrity**: Never imitate or reproduce another studio's protected artistic style.
3. **Budget**: Stay strictly within the approved budget of ₹1,500 maximum per episode.
4. **Preservation**: Preserve V001 as the foundation. Do not rewrite V001. Apply Version 2.0 beginning with V002.

## Narrative & Voice Strategy
1. **Narration & Dialogue**:
   * Do not make multi-character voice generation mandatory.
   * Use narration strategically and reserve character dialogue for emotionally important scenes until voice consistency across episodes is proven.
   * Narration should only be used when absolutely necessary to bridge time or location.
   * Target ratio: 80% cinematic scenes, 20% narration.
2. **Dialogue**: Dialogue must feel natural and avoid exposition. Characters should never explain what the audience already sees.

## Video-First Workflow (LOCKED)
The image-first workflow is deprecated. Stop generating image production plans. The primary production asset is a video clip, not a still image. Only use still images when a scene cannot be economically generated as video. Our target is that viewers feel they are watching an animated film rather than a slideshow.

For every scene, produce:
1. **Google Flow prompt**
2. **Scene objective**
3. **Character dialogue**
4. **Camera movement**
5. **Lighting**
6. **Sound design**
7. **Music cue**
8. **Scene duration (6–10 seconds)**
9. **Ending frame continuity for the next scene**

## Episode Design & Performance
Every episode must define the following sections:

### YouTube Performance Map
*   **3 Hero Moments**: High-impact visual or dramatic turning points.
*   **5 Emotional Moments**: Scenes designed specifically to evoke strong emotions (fear, wonder, devotion, sadness).
*   **1 Thumbnail Moment**: The key visual beat designed specifically to capture clicks.
*   **1 Viral Moment**: A high-impact, shareable statement, revelation, or scene.
*   **1 Shorts Moment**: A self-contained, high-retention 30–60 second segment.

### Viewer Retention Map
Planned engagement beats mapping the tension and curiosity from the opening hook through the ending teaser:
*   **Hook (0:00 - 0:15)**: Instant curiosity trigger/dramatic question.
*   **Engagement Beat 1**: First narrative peak.
*   **Engagement Beat 2**: Second narrative peak / rising action.
*   **Climax**: Highest tension point.
*   **Resolution & Lesson**: Relatable takeaway.
*   **Teaser**: Ending hook for the next episode.

## Scene Design Criteria
Every scene must specify:
1. Scene objective
2. Emotional objective ("What should the audience FEEL?")
3. Camera language (Wide establishing, close emotional, OTS, slow push-in, dolly, crane reveal, orbit, silhouette, foreground framing, depth)
4. Lighting (Volumetric, twilight, warm golden, etc.)
5. Sound design (Rain, fire, chains, footsteps, conch, thunder, etc.)
6. Dialogue
7. Music direction (Percussion, choir, strings, flute, etc.)
8. Visual effects (Particle overlays, lighting animation, etc.)
9. Transition (Cross dissolve, hard cut, etc.)
10. Viewer emotion
