## 1. OVERALL VISUAL PHILOSOPHY

### The Three Pillars:

**PILLAR 1: Sacred Realism**
> We render the divine world as if it ACTUALLY EXISTS — not as cartoon,
> not as abstract art, but as a hyper-real place that feels more vivid
> than reality itself. Like stepping into a dream that's more real than
> waking life.

**PILLAR 2: Cinematic Reverence**
> Every frame is composed with the intentionality of a Renaissance painting
> and the drama of a Hollywood epic. No frame is accidental. No shot is
> "just filler." If a frame can't stand alone as art, it doesn't belong.

**PILLAR 3: Emotional Truth**
> Visuals serve FEELING first, information second. The viewer should FEEL
> the story in their chest before their mind processes what happened.
> Color, light, and composition are emotional tools.

### Reference Influences:
- **Pixar** — Emotional lighting, character expressiveness
- **Roger Deakins** — Naturalistic cinematography, motivated light
- **Raja Ravi Varma** — Indian divine portraiture, draped fabrics
- **Kurzgesagt** — Visual clarity, bold color choices
- **Terrence Malick** — Golden hour reverence, nature as character
- **Traditional Pichwai paintings** — Krishna iconography, floral motifs
- **Ajanta caves** — Ancient Indian visual grammar

### The LEELA Look (One Sentence):
> "A Pixar film set in ancient India, shot by Roger Deakins,
> painted by Raja Ravi Varma."

## 2. ART STYLE

### Style Definition: **Cinematic Sacred Realism**

| Parameter | Specification |
|-----------|--------------|
| Rendering | Photorealistic with 10% painterly softness |
| Proportions | Anatomically accurate, slightly idealized (divine beauty) |
| Detail level | High in foreground, soft/atmospheric in background |
| Texture | Rich fabric textures, skin pores visible, nature detail |
| Atmosphere | Always present — dust, fog, light rays, particles |
| Scale | Characters feel grounded in environment (not floating) |

### What This Is:
- Photorealistic Indian mythological art
- Characters have weight, presence, and physicality
- Environments feel lived-in and ancient
- Divine elements (auras, particles) integrated naturally
- Fabric drapes with real physics
- Light behaves realistically with divine enhancement

### What This Is NOT:
- Cartoon/anime style
- Flat illustration
- Oversaturated "calendar art"
- Dark/horror aesthetic
- Western fantasy (no European castles, no pale skin)
- AI "plastic" look (avoid uncanny smooth skin)

### Texture Philosophy:
```
Skin:       Subtle subsurface scattering, visible pores, warm undertones
Fabric:     Thread-level detail on silk, natural draping, catch-light on folds
Metal:      Reflective but not chrome — aged gold, burnished bronze
Stone:      Weathered, moss-touched, ancient
Wood:       Grain visible, warm tones, hand-carved detail
Water:      Crystal clear with reflections, slight blue-green tint
```

## 4. LIGHTING STYLE

### Philosophy: "Motivated Divine Light"

All light has a SOURCE — even divine light comes FROM somewhere (Krishna's body, the sky, a lamp, the sun). Light never appears without reason.

### Primary Lighting Setups:

**Type 1: Temple Light (Default)**
```
Key:        Warm golden sidelight (from oil lamps/windows)
Fill:       Cool blue ambient (moonlight or sky)
Rim:        Subtle golden edge light (divine)
Ratio:      3:1 (dramatic but readable)
Shadows:    Soft, never pitch black
```

**Type 2: Forest Light (Vrindavan)**
```
Key:        Dappled sunlight through trees (god rays)
Fill:       Green-tinted bounce from foliage
Rim:        Backlit golden hair/body edge
Volumetric: Visible light shafts in fog/dust
Shadows:    Complex, natural, leaf-patterns
```

**Type 3: Divine Light (Miracles)**
```
Key:        Emanating FROM Krishna's body (warm white-gold)
Fill:       Cool blue push from opposite
Rim:        Intense golden glow (almost overexposed edge)
Bloom:      Subtle light bloom on divine source
Particles:  Golden motes floating in light beams
```

**Type 4: War Light (Kurukshetra)**
```
Key:        Harsh, directional sunlight (amber/orange)
Fill:       Deep shadows, minimal fill
Rim:        Dust-filtered backlight (volumetric)
Atmosphere: Thick dust catching directional beams
Mood:       High contrast, apocalyptic
```

**Type 5: Night Light (Romance/Mystery)**
```
Key:        Moonlight (cool blue-white, directional)
Fill:       Firefly warmth / oil lamp (localized)
Rim:        Silver moonlight on hair/shoulders
Stars:      Subtle point lights in background
Mood:       Intimate, quiet, vast
```

### Lighting Rules:
1. **Krishna ALWAYS has subtle divine rim light** — even in shadows
2. **Never flat lighting** — there must always be a light-to-shadow transition
3. **Golden hour is preferred default** — warm, flattering, spiritual
4. **Divine moments = light intensifies** — as if the sun responded to the divine
5. **Villain scenes = lower key ratio** — deeper shadows, cooler tones

## 6. CAMERA LANGUAGE

### Shot Type = Emotional Meaning:

| Shot | Usage | Emotional Effect |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Extreme Wide | Establishing locations, showing scale | Viewer feels small, awe |
| Wide | Action scenes, group interactions | Context, geography |
| Medium | Conversations, storytelling moments | Connection, narrative flow |
| Close-up | Emotional peaks, revelations | Intimacy, importance |
| Extreme Close-up | Eyes, hands, objects of significance | Maximum emotional impact |
| Over-the-shoulder | Dialogue between characters | Immersion in conversation |
| Low angle (looking up) | Krishna in power, divine moments | Authority, reverence |
| High angle (looking down) | Vulnerability, smallness | Sympathy, helplessness |
| Bird's eye | Battlefield overview, cosmic scale | God's perspective |
| Dutch angle | NEVER USE | — |

### When to Use Each:

```
HOOK (0:00-0:15):      Close-up OR Extreme Wide (maximum impact)
STORY SETUP:           Wide → Medium (orient → engage)
EMOTIONAL PEAK:        Slow push to Close-up (build intensity)
DIVINE MOMENT:         Low angle + slow zoom out (reveal grandeur)
BATTLE:                Wide → Quick medium cuts (chaos + clarity)
LESSON/WISDOM:         Medium, stable (respect, attention)
CLOSING:               Slow pull-back to wide (release, peace)
```

## 7. LENS GUIDE

### Simulated Focal Lengths:

| Focal Length | Usage | Characteristics |
|-------------|-------|-----------------|
| 14-24mm (Ultra Wide) | Cosmic scenes, Vishwaroop, palace interiors | Dramatic scale, slight distortion at edges |
| 35mm | Establishing shots, environments | Natural perspective, grounded |
| 50mm (Standard) | Default for narration scenes | Most natural to human eye |
| 85mm | Portraits, emotional close-ups | Beautiful background blur, flattering |
| 135mm | Intimate moments, isolating subjects | Creamy bokeh, compression |
| 200mm+ | Battlefield with distant armies, wildlife | Extreme compression, epic layers |

### Depth of Field Rules:
```
Shallow (f/1.4-2.8):   Emotional close-ups, single subject focus
Medium (f/4-5.6):      Two characters interacting, environmental portrait
Deep (f/8-16):         Landscapes, battle panoramas, establishing shots
Rack focus:            Use for dramatic attention shift (A → B reveals)
```

### Lens Character:
- **Always warm** — simulate vintage glass with slight amber coating
- **Subtle lens flares** — only for divine light sources (golden, not blue)
- **No chromatic aberration** — keep it clean and premium
- **Gentle vignetting** — 10-15% darker corners (draws eye to center)

## 8. CAMERA MOVEMENT GUIDE

### Movement = Emotion Chart:

| Movement | Speed | When | Emotion |
|----------|-------|------|---------|
| Static (locked) | 0 | Dialogue, wisdom, important statements | Stability, truth, authority |
| Slow push in | Very slow | Building tension, approaching importance | Growing intensity |
| Slow pull out | Very slow | Reveal, resolution, showing context | Release, understanding |
| Pan (horizontal) | Slow | Scanning landscapes, following action | Journey, exploration |
| Tilt (vertical) | Slow | Revealing tall structures, divine forms | Awe, scale |
| Ken Burns (photo pan) | Very slow | Still images, paintings | Gentle life to static art |
| Dolly (forward/back) | Medium | Entering/leaving a space | Immersion/withdrawal |
| Crane (up) | Slow | Endings, divine ascension | Transcendence |
| Handheld/Shake | NEVER | — | — (too casual for this brand) |
| Whip pan | NEVER | — | — (too modern/trendy) |

### Speed Rules:
```
PEACEFUL scenes:     Camera moves at 2-5% per second (barely perceptible)
EMOTIONAL scenes:    Camera moves at 5-10% (noticeable but gentle)
DRAMATIC scenes:     Camera moves at 10-20% (deliberate and powerful)
ACTION scenes:       Camera moves at 20-40% (dynamic but controlled)
NEVER:               Instant movement or jerky motion
```

### The LEELA Camera Rule:
> "The camera moves like a devotee in a temple —
> slowly, reverently, with intention and grace."

## 9. COMPOSITION RULES

### Primary Composition Framework: Rule of Sacred Thirds

```
┌─────────┬─────────┬─────────┐
│         │         │         │
│    ●    │    ●    │    ●    │ ← Power points (place eyes/focus here)
│         │         │         │
├─────────┼─────────┼─────────┤
│         │         │         │
│    ●    │  CENTER │    ●    │ ← Center reserved for divine/cosmic ONLY
│         │         │         │
├─────────┼─────────┼─────────┤
│         │         │         │
│    ●    │    ●    │    ●    │
│         │         │         │
└─────────┴─────────┴─────────┘
```

### Composition Principles:

**1. The Divine Center Rule:**
- Center framing is ONLY for Krishna in full divine glory (Vishwaroop, supreme moment)
- All other subjects: off-center (rule of thirds)
- Center = sacred. Use it rarely for maximum impact.

**2. Leading Space:**
- Characters always have space in front of them (direction they face/move)
- Never crowd a character against frame edge

**3. Depth Layers (Mandatory):**
```
Every shot must have THREE depth layers:
FOREGROUND:  Blurred element (flowers, fabric, pillar edge)
MIDGROUND:   Subject (character, action)
BACKGROUND:  Environment (sky, architecture, nature)
```

**4. Triangular Composition:**
- Group scenes use triangular arrangements
- Krishna at apex of triangle (highest/most prominent)
- Supporting characters form base

**5. Diagonal Energy:**
- Battle scenes: strong diagonals (tension, movement)
- Peace scenes: horizontal dominance (calm, stability)
- Divine scenes: vertical dominance (aspiration, transcendence)

**6. Negative Space:**
- Always allow breathing room (never overcrowd)
- Sky/empty space above divine figures = grandeur
- Emptiness can be more powerful than detail

## 10. BACKGROUND STYLE

### Philosophy: Backgrounds Are Characters

Backgrounds are never just "behind" the subject. They tell their own story — the culture, the era, the emotion, the divinity of the world.

### Environmental Layers:
```
Layer 1 (Closest):   Ground detail — grass, flowers, scattered petals, stone
Layer 2 (Near):      Props — pots, carts, instruments, tools
Layer 3 (Mid):       Architecture — huts, gates, pillars
Layer 4 (Far):       Landscape — hills, forests, river bends
Layer 5 (Sky):       Atmosphere — clouds, birds, divine phenomena
```

### Key Rule: Every Background Has Life
- Birds always in the sky
- Leaves always gently moving
- Dust or pollen always in the air
- Flowers or nature always present
- Nothing feels sterile or empty

## 11. VILLAGE ARCHITECTURE (Gokul/Vrindavan)

### Visual Characteristics:
```
Structure:      Mud and clay walls, thatched roofs (dried grass/palm)
Colors:         Warm earth tones — terracotta, cream, ochre, sandstone
Doors:          Wooden, carved, small (human-scale architecture)
Courtyards:     Open central spaces with tulsi plant, cow dung floor
Fences:         Bamboo or rough wood, irregular, handmade feel
Streets:        Narrow, winding, unpaved (packed earth)
Details:        Rangoli on thresholds, hanging marigold strings
                Small diyas (oil lamps) in niches
                Water pots (matkas) near doors
                Cow dung cakes drying on walls
Trees:          Banyan (massive), Peepal, Neem growing into structures
```

### Vrindavan-Specific:
```
Yamuna ghats:   Stepped stone edges leading to river
Kadamba trees:  Where Krishna plays flute (tall, spreading canopy)
Govardhan hill: In background — rounded green hill
Nand Bhavan:    Slightly larger house, still humble but prosperous
                Butter pots hanging from ceiling beams
```

### Architecture DON'Ts:
- No modern materials (no concrete, no glass, no metal roofing)
- No perfectly straight walls (handmade = slightly imperfect)
- No western architectural elements
- No empty walls — always textures, wear, decoration

## 13. RIVER DESIGN (Yamuna)

### Yamuna River Characteristics:
```
Water Color:    Deep blue-green, NOT muddy, crystal clear near banks
Flow:           Gentle, meandering (never rapids in story scenes)
Width:          Variable — narrow enough to see both banks in some scenes
Ghats:          Stone steps leading into water, worn smooth by time
Banks:          Sandy with river pebbles, grass at edges
Lotuses:        Floating on surface — white and pink varieties
Reflections:    ALWAYS show sky/tree reflections (shows water quality)
Light:          Water catches and scatters divine golden light
```

### River Scenes:
```
Morning bathing:    Mist rising, golden sunrise reflected
Rasa Leela:         Full moon reflecting, silver water, fireflies
Krishna childhood:  Shallow pools, playful splashing, clear water
Emotional:          Still water, perfect reflections (mirror of soul)
```

### Water Rendering Rules:
- Water is NEVER opaque (always some transparency at edges)
- Ripples from movement are subtle (not splashy)
- Lotus stems visible below surface
- Fish occasionally visible (life in water)
- Moon/sun reflection stretches naturally

## 15. TEMPLE DESIGN

### Temple Visual Language:
```
Style:          Nagara (North Indian) temple architecture
Material:       Sandstone (warm gold-brown) or white marble
Shikhara:       Tall, curved tower with amalaka (disc) on top
Mandapa:        Pillared hall with carved ceilings
Sanctum:        Dark interior, single oil lamp lighting deity
Steps:          Wide, leading up (physical ascent = spiritual ascent)
Carvings:       Every surface has sculptural detail (gods, dancers, nature)
Bells:          Hanging brass bells at entrance
```

### Temple Atmosphere:
```
Interior:       Dark ambient + warm lamp light, incense smoke visible
Exterior:       Full sunlight, dramatic against blue sky
Sound implied:  Bells, chanting, birdsong from outside
Floor:          Cool stone with flower petals scattered
Fragrance:      Imply through incense smoke trails, flower garlands
```

### Temple at Different Times:
- **Dawn:** Misty, first light catching shikhara top, birds circling
- **Day:** Bold contrast, deep shadows in doorways, warm stone
- **Night:** Oil lamps on every step, orange glow against blue-black sky
- **Festival:** Draped in marigold garlands, oil lamps everywhere, crowds

Material:       Fine muslin or silk — lightweight, flowing

Material:       Leather base + metal plates (bronze/gold)
Color:          Character-coded (Pandava = gold/blue, Kaurava = dark)

```
Material:       Rough cotton, handwoven, simple
Colors:         Earth tones — off-white, brown, muted green, indigo

Silk:       High specularity, golden catch-lights, rich shadows

```

## 18. SKIN RENDERING

### Krishna's Blue Skin:
```
Base:           Deep cosmic blue (#1e3a8a to #1d4ed8)
Subsurface:     Warm purple-magenta visible in thin areas (ears, fingers)
Highlights:     Cool blue-white (reflected sky/divine light)
Shadows:        Deep indigo-purple (NOT black, NOT grey)
Glow:           Subtle radiance — skin seems to emit soft light
Texture:        Smooth but with realistic micro-detail (not plastic)
Consistency:    EXACT SAME SHADE in every video (brand critical)
```

**Important:** Krishna's blue is NOT paint. It's his SKIN COLOR. Render it with the same subsurface scattering and life as any skin — just in blue.

### Radha/Human Skin (Indian)
```
Base:           Warm golden-brown (range: fair #deb887 to medium #a0522d)
Subsurface:     Warm red-orange in cheeks, ears, fingertips
Highlights:     Warm golden (reflecting Indian sunlight)
Shadows:        Warm brown (never cold grey)
Texture:        Smooth but alive — subtle pores, natural sheen
Blush:          Warm rose on cheeks for emotion
```

### Villain Skin (Kansa, Demons):
```
Base:           Desaturated, slightly greyish undertone
Texture:        Rougher, more pronounced features
Light:          Harsher, less flattering angles
Contrast:       Higher contrast (more dramatic shadows)
```

### Skin Rules:
1. NO pure white highlights on skin (always tinted with environment)
2. NO pitch-black shadows on skin (always some subsurface bounce)
3. Skin in shadow is NOT just "darker" — it shifts in hue (more purple/blue)
4. Sweat/moisture = scene-dependent (battle yes, divine moment no)
5. Children's skin is smoother with more subsurface scatter (translucency)

## 19. HAIR RENDERING

### Krishna's Hair:
```
Color:          Deep blue-black (almost blends with skin in shadow)
Style:          Long, slightly curly, flowing past shoulders
Crown area:     Tied partially up with peacock feather tucked in
Loose strands:  Few playful strands near face (especially as child)
Movement:       Gentle, continuous — as if divine wind always present
Shine:          Blue-black highlights (like raven feathers)
Child Krishna:  Shorter, curlier, more playful
```

### Radha's Hair:
```
Color:          Rich deep black-brown
Style:          Long, thick, flowing, partially braided
Adornments:     Flowers (jasmine strings), gold pins, maang tikka chain
Movement:       Graceful, slower than Krishna's (more gravity, femininity)
Shine:          Warm golden-brown highlights in sunlight
Loose strands:  Near temples for intimacy, fully styled for formal scenes
```

### Hair Rendering Rules:
1. Hair is NEVER uniform solid color (always gradient, always highlight/shadow)
2. Wind affects hair SUBTLY — never chaotic (grace, not chaos)

## 39. SCENE TRANSITION RULES

### Permitted Transitions:

| Transition | Usage | Duration | Visual |
|-----------|-------|----------|--------|
| Cross dissolve | Default between scenes | 1.5-2s | Gentle overlap |
| Fade to black | End of chapter/major time skip | 2-3s | Full black momentarily |
| Fade from black | Beginning new chapter/section | 2-3s | Emerging from darkness |
| Light flash (white/gold) | Divine moment, miracle, revelation | 0.3-0.5s | Flash + bloom |
| Match cut | Visual rhyming (object → similar object) | 0s (direct cut) | Seamless shape/color match |
| Whip to blur | RARE — only for fast travel/time jump | 0.5s | Horizontal blur |
| Particle wipe | Transition via golden particles | 1.5s | Particles sweep across frame |

### Transition Emotional Logic:
```
Peaceful → Peaceful:        Cross dissolve (smooth continuation)
Peaceful → Dramatic:        Hard cut (shock) or fade to black → emerge
Dramatic → Dramatic:        Hard cut (keep energy) or match cut
Dramatic → Peaceful:        Slow fade to black → emerge gently
Any → Divine:               Light flash (marks supernatural entry)
Divine → Normal:            Slow dissolve with particles fading
```

### Transition Rules:
1. **NEVER use more than 2 transition types in any 60-second span**
2. **Hard cuts for DIALOGUE** — conversation scenes use simple cuts, never dissolves
3. **Cross dissolves for NARRATION** — when narrator is driving (no on-screen dialogue)
4. **Every transition has EMOTIONAL purpose** — if you can't name why, use hard cut
5. **The sacred transitions (light flash, particle wipe) max 2-3 per video**

**NEVER in Motion:**
```
❌ Handheld/shaky camera (even simulated)
❌ Jump cuts (jarring, disrespectful to flow)
❌ Whip pans (too modern/trendy)
❌ Speed ramps for comedy (tone violation)
❌ Zoom punches (YouTube-gamer-style zoom)
❌ Split screen (divides attention, breaks immersion)
❌ Picture-in-picture (this is cinema, not a reaction video)
```

**NEVER in Color:**
```
❌ Full desaturation/black-and-white (divine world is always vivid)
❌ Neon colors (unnatural, breaks sacred aesthetic)
❌ Cool/teal-and-orange Hollywood grading (not Indian)
❌ Random color shifts without emotional motivation
❌ Different color grading between shots in same scene
```

**NEVER in Content:**
```
❌ Depicting Krishna as confused, weak, or foolish
❌ Showing disrespect to any deity (even villains are not mocked)
❌ Political symbolism or modern commentary through visuals
❌ Celebrity likenesses for divine characters
❌ AI-generated "weird" artifacts (extra fingers, melted faces) — QC strictly
❌ Inconsistency with brand bible (this document is law)
```
