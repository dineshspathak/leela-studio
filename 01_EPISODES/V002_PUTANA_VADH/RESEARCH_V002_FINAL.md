# 🔬 RESEARCH — V002: पूतना वध की कथा (The Liberation of Putana)

## Research Document — NOT a Script
### Sources: Bhagavata Purana (Canto 10, Ch 6), Vishnu Purana (Book V, Ch 5), Harivamsa (Ch 58)

## 1. TIMELINE (Chronological Sequence of Events)

| # | Event | Source | Details |
|---|-------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Kamsa decrees the slaughter of all newborns in the kingdom | SB 10.6.1 | Following Yogamaya's warning, Kamsa orders all infants born in the last 10 days to be killed. |
| 2 | Putana, a demoness with the power to fly, is dispatched to Gokul | SB 10.6.2 | Putana (a child-killer demoness / *Balagraha*) specializes in poisoning infants. |
| 3 | Putana enters Gokul disguised as a beautiful, glowing woman | SB 10.6.9-10 | She styles herself with flowers in her hair, large hips, and a gentle smile. The residents mistake her for Goddess Lakshmi. |
| 4 | She enters Nanda's house unchallenged | SB 10.6.11-12 | Yashoda and Rohini are present but are spellbound by her radiant, motherly disguise and do not stop her. |
| 5 | Putana sees baby Krishna in the cradle | SB 10.6.13 | She recognizes his divine power but hides her malice. Krishna closes his eyes as she approaches. |
| 6 | She picks up Krishna and offers her breast smeared with poison | SB 10.6.15 | Her breasts are smeared with *kalkuta* (deadly poison). She acts with maternal affection to deceive the onlookers. |
| 7 | Krishna sucks her breast, drawing out both the poison and her life | SB 10.6.17 | Krishna squeezes her breast with his hands and sucks with great force, absorbing her *prana* (life force). |
| 8 | Putana screams in agonizing pain, her disguise dissolving | SB 10.6.18 | She cries "Let go, let go!" (*muñca muñca*). Her scream is so loud it shakes the earth and sky. |
| 9 | Her giant demoness body falls dead outside Gokul, crushing trees | SB 10.6.21-22 | Her original form is revealed: a massive demoness with fangs, long hair, and limbs like tree trunks. |
| 10 | Gokul residents rush out and find Krishna playing on her chest | SB 10.6.23-24 | The Gopis quickly rescue baby Krishna, completely unharmed, laughing on the corpse. |
| 11 | Gopis perform protective cleansing rituals for the baby | SB 10.6.25-29 | They bathe Krishna, wave a cow's tail over him, and apply dust, cow dung, and urine while chanting the names of Vishnu. |
| 12 | Putana's body is chopped up and cremated | SB 10.6.33-34 | Because Krishna sucked her milk, she was purified of all sins. Fragrant wood-smoke rises from her pyre, smelling of sandalwood. |
| 13 | Putana is granted motherly liberation (moksha) | SB 10.6.35-44 | Because she offered her breast (even with malice), Krishna accepted her as a foster mother and granted her a place in the spiritual world. |

## 2. CHARACTERS

*   **Shri Krishna (Baby)**: Luminous cosmic blue infant. Appears helpless but is omniscient. Closes his eyes to: (1) refuse to see the demoness's sin, or (2) act as the consumer of the world's poison.
*   **Putana**: 
    *   *Disguise Form*: Impossibly beautiful, wearing a saffron saree, jasmine flowers in her hair, glowing skin, kind eyes.
    *   *Demoness Form*: Giant (multi-mile long), fangs, tangled red hair, dark grey-green skin, hollow eyes.
*   **Yashoda**: Devastated by the event, protective. Bathes Krishna in cow dung/urine to ward off evil.
*   **Rohini**: Mother of Balarama, helper in the protective rituals.
*   **Nanda Maharaja**: Returns from Mathura just as the corpse falls, amazed by the realization of Vasudeva's warnings.

## 3. KEY THEOLOGICAL NUANCES (For Scriptwriting)

1.  **Why Krishna Closed His Eyes**: Acharyas explain that Krishna closed his eyes because:
    *   He did not want to look at the face of a child-killer.
    *   He wanted to meditate on how to grant liberation to someone who came to murder him.
    *   As the Lord of Death, his glance would destroy her instantly, but he wanted to suck her breast first.
2.  **The Sandalwood Smoke Miracle**: The cremation of a demoness usually smells of rotting flesh. However, Putana's pyre smelled of sweet sandalwood because her contact with Krishna purified her entire existence. This is the central emotional pivot: *even the greatest malice is converted into maternal liberation by the touch of the Lord.*
